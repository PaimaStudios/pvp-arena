Weapon Types

  • Axe — Pure crusher. Highest crush damage per hand (17). Zero defense. Pair with a shield offhand for survivability, or dual-wield for maximum aggression.
  • Shield — Purely defensive. Massive defense on both types (35 crush / 38 pierce). Negligible damage. Always offhand — dual shields are forbidden. Frees your mainhand to be anything.
  • Sword — Balanced generalist. Some pierce damage, some crush damage, good dex, and the only weapon with built-in crush defense. Works in almost any build.
  • Spear — Pierce specialist with a defensive edge. High pierce damage plus pierce defense. Slow dex (1) — don't count on agility.
  • Bow — Highest single-weapon damage (42 pierce), two-handed. The empty other hand grants +18 DEX, making bow heroes the fastest. Glass cannon: zero defenses.
  • Unarmed — Each empty hand gives +18 DEX. Only effective if you can exploit weight differences against heavy opponents. Fringe dex build.

Armor Types

  • Leather — Better against crush (6 def), lighter (weight 3). Keeps your dex strong. Best for agile or bow builds.
  • Metal — Better against pierce (8 def), heavier (weight 7). Tanks pierce damage but makes you a dex target. Best against bow and spear builds.
Chest armor is 3× more impactful than helmet or greaves. Skirt is 2×. Always prioritize your chest slot.

Quick Build Guides

⚔ Berserker

Axe + Axe. Leather chest & skirt. Play Aggressive every round. Melts heroes with no crush defense.

🛡 Tank

Shield offhand + Sword or Spear. Full metal chest & skirt. Defensive under pressure, Neutral otherwise. Wins wars of attrition.

🏹 Sniper

Bow (2-handed). No armor or leather only. 42 pierce + 18 dex punishes every round. Fragile — one focused attacker drops you fast.

🗡 Brawler

Sword + Shield. Leather chest. Neutral/Defensive stance. A safer berserker that survives longer.

💨 Dex Ghost

Unarmed both hands or Bow. Zero/minimal armor. Beats heavy metal opponents. Loses to other light builds.

Stance Play

Every round each hero picks a stance — this is your main decision point. Your stance affects both the damage you deal and the damage you receive.

Target Selection

You assign each of your 3 heroes a target each round. Dead heroes stop dealing damage, so:

  • Focus fire one enemy hero to eliminate them quickly and even the numbers.
  • Spread fire if all enemies are equally threatening — rarely optimal.
  • Protect your key hero by anticipating where the enemy will focus — put a defensive build in that spot.

The Commit-Reveal Edge

Your moves are hidden until P1 reveals — P2 sees P1's commit hash but not the actual moves.

  • As P2 — you're effectively guessing P1's targets and stance. Mix your stances if unsure rather than committing to one.
  • As P1 — you commit blind. If you suspect P2 will go Aggressive, Defensive stance can neutralize their burst round.

Damage Formula

damage = ( (100 - enemy.crush_def) × attacker.crush_dmg + (100 - enemy.pierce_def) × attacker.pierce_dmg + (50 + enemy.weight - attacker.weight) × attacker.dex_bonus ) × (stance_mod(attacker) + stance_mod(defender))

Stance modifiers: Aggressive = 8, Neutral = 5, Defensive = 2.
A hero dies when accumulated damage reaches 100,000.

What This Means

Defense is a percentage reduction applied before multipliers. 35 crush_def means the attacker's crush damage is multiplied by 65 instead of 100. Against a 17-crush axe, that's 1700 → 1105 crush contribution per exchange.

Dex scales with weight delta. The term (50 + enemy.weight - your.weight):

  • Equal weights → ×50 multiplier on dex
  • Enemy full metal (~42 weight) vs your naked hero (0) → ×92 multiplier
  • You full metal vs naked enemy → ×8 multiplier — your dex is nearly worthless

Stances are additive, not multiplicative. Aggressive vs Aggressive (16×) deals 4× more damage than Defensive vs Defensive (4×) — the gap compounds over multiple rounds.
